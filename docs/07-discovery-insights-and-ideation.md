## 7. Discovery Insights & Ideation Notes

This section captures deeper discovery insights, hypotheses, and early opportunity framing from the exploratory `IDEAS` document.

---

### 7.1 Core Observations

- People who cannot access the formal rental market are forced into fragmented informal channels where critical information, such as administrative compatibility, is unclear.
- The biggest challenge is not finding listings — it is finding a viable living situation under real-life constraints.

---

### 7.2 Hypotheses About Trust and Informal Markets

- In informal housing markets, trust is often established through social connections rather than through the platform itself.
- Some of the most accessible housing opportunities do not appear on major real estate platforms. Instead, they emerge through smaller social channels, subletting situations, or niche communities where competition is lower and landlords may have more flexible requirements.
- Discovering these opportunities requires constant monitoring of fragmented channels and often involves uncertainty regarding trust and legitimacy.

---

### 7.3 Response and Messaging Dynamics

**Response bottleneck in housing platforms**

- Users often send dozens of messages to landlords or tenants, but response rates remain low.
- This creates a high-effort interaction loop where users must continuously send outreach messages without knowing whether they will receive a response.

**Uncertain response probability**

- Platforms may show how many people have contacted a landlord, but not whether the landlord is still actively responding.
- Users continue sending messages even when the probability of receiving a response is low, turning the search into a game of chance.

**Template messaging behavior**

- Due to high competition and low response rates, users often adopt a template messaging strategy.
- Outreach volume becomes more important than meaningful interaction.

---

### 7.4 Landlord and Tenant Segmentation

- Not all landlords behave the same way:
  - Individual landlords or temporary sublets may be more flexible and responsive.
  - Professional landlords or agencies may have stricter requirements.
- Freelancers, artists, and recently relocated individuals often struggle to meet formal requirements and rely more heavily on informal channels.
- Higher-income digital nomads may still access the formal market due to their ability to meet financial requirements.

---

### 7.5 Informal Subletting Market & Low-Visibility Supply

- A significant portion of temporary housing opportunities exists in an informal subletting market.
- These opportunities are often not listed on traditional platforms because contracts do not formally allow subletting.
- Opportunities are shared through:
  - Social media pages
  - Personal networks
  - Small, niche channels
- This creates a gap between **total housing supply** and **usable housing supply** for newcomers.

---

### 7.6 Housing as Administrative Gateway

- For recently arrived immigrants, housing is a gateway to administrative integration.
- Without a valid address that allows **empadronamiento**, individuals may struggle to:
  - Access healthcare
  - Complete legal documentation
  - Access other public services
- Housing becomes the first major barrier to integration.

---

### 7.7 Temporary Landing Phase & Preparedness

- Many newcomers go through a temporary landing phase (1–2 weeks), staying in:
  - Friends’ homes
  - Short-term rentals
  - Hotels
- This phase creates pressure to secure accommodation quickly.
- Preparedness levels vary:
  - Some arrive with accommodation pre-booked.
  - Others arrive with little preparation and higher vulnerability to unreliable opportunities.

---

### 7.8 Administrative Compatibility as Primary Filter

- Empadronamiento is a critical requirement for many newcomers.
- Listings that do not allow empadronamiento may block access to essential services.
- Decision hierarchy for these users:
  1. Administrative compatibility  
  2. Location fit  
  3. Property quality  
  4. Contact / response  

---

### 7.9 Empadronamiento as Scarce Resource

- Empadronamiento can function like a scarce administrative resource.
- Some landlords or tenants:
  - Avoid confirming it openly in listings.
  - May charge additional fees.
  - Provide vague or conditional responses such as “maybe”.

---

### 7.10 Transitional Housing as Administrative Bridge

- Short-term housing (e.g., 1–3 months) often serves as an administrative bridge.
- It allows newcomers to:
  - Register an address
  - Access essential services
  - Search for longer-term housing from a more stable position

---

### 7.11 Core Discovery Insight

For many newcomers arriving in Barcelona, the primary goal is not securing the perfect long-term apartment but achieving **short-term administrative stability**.

Transitional housing with administrative compatibility (empadronamiento) becomes significantly more valuable than traditional listings during the first phase of relocation.

Users value:

- Curated opportunities over high volume
- Clear signals (empadronamiento, recency, responsiveness, basic trust) over raw listing quantity
