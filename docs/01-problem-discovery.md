## 1. Problem Discovery

This section describes the context of the housing problem in Barcelona, who is affected, and how people currently search for housing.

---

### Problem

Barcelona is a cosmopolitan city that attracts international students, digital nomads, expatriates, and immigrants every year. However, finding housing in the city has become increasingly difficult for both newcomers and local residents.

Beyond the high demand for housing, the process of searching for an apartment is often confusing and inefficient. The information needed to make a decision is fragmented across multiple platforms, and listings frequently lack clear or complete details.

For many newcomers, the situation is even more complex due to administrative requirements such as **empadronamiento**, which is often necessary to access public services and establish legal residency.

As a result, finding an apartment in Barcelona becomes a slow, stressful, and highly manual process for people who are already navigating relocation and bureaucracy in a new country.

---

### Who Is Affected by This Problem

The housing challenges in Barcelona affect multiple groups, although their needs and constraints differ.

#### Primary Users

People relocating to Barcelona, including:

- International students (e.g., Erasmus)
- Digital nomads
- International professionals
- Immigrants arriving in Spain

These users often require **empadronamiento** to access healthcare, obtain documentation such as the **NIE**, and integrate into the local administrative system.

#### Secondary Users

Local residents in Barcelona also experience housing difficulties due to:

- high housing demand
- rising rental prices
- limited supply of affordable apartments

However, their constraints are typically different, as they are already integrated into the local administrative system.

---

### Current Search Behavior

People searching for housing in Barcelona rely on a combination of real estate platforms and informal digital channels.

#### Real Estate Platforms

Most users begin their search on traditional property websites such as:

- Idealista  
- Fotocasa  
- Habitaclia  

These platforms provide a large number of listings, but users often need to manually contact landlords or real estate agencies to confirm important details about the apartment.

#### Room and Shared Housing Platforms

For people looking for shared apartments or rooms, platforms such as:

- Badi  

are commonly used. These platforms facilitate room rentals but still require direct communication with landlords or tenants to clarify conditions.

#### Social Media Channels

Many housing opportunities are also shared through informal channels, including:

- Facebook housing groups  
- Instagram pages posting available rooms and apartments  

These channels can sometimes provide faster opportunities, but they often lack standardized information and may increase the risk of unreliable listings.

---

### Additional Discovery Insight: Price vs Value

During the discovery phase, another important pattern emerged in the housing search experience.

Apartment prices alone do not clearly represent the real value of a listing. In the same neighborhood in Barcelona, listings with similar prices may represent very different types of housing:

- a shared room in a crowded apartment
- a small studio
- a full apartment in poor condition
- a larger or better-maintained property

As a result, users often struggle to understand whether a listing is actually a **good opportunity or an overpriced option**.

Many listings also lack clear information about:

- real size of the room or apartment
- quality of the property
- included amenities (e.g., air conditioning, furniture)
- number of people sharing the apartment

This forces users to manually analyze photos, descriptions, and location details to determine if the price reflects the actual value of the property.

---

### Typical User Flow

1. Search across multiple platforms and social networks  
2. Open and review dozens of listings  
3. Read descriptions to identify relevant details  
4. Contact landlords or agencies directly  
5. Ask follow-up questions to confirm conditions and requirements  
6. Evaluate whether the listing is legitimate  
7. Schedule visits if the opportunity appears trustworthy

---

> 💡 **Key Insight**
>
> Searching for housing in Barcelona requires navigating multiple platforms, contacting landlords or agencies directly, and manually verifying critical information.  
> The process is fragmented, time-consuming, and often involves uncertainty about the reliability of listings.

---

### Key Challenges

- Housing information is fragmented across multiple platforms and social media channels  
- Important details are often hidden within listing descriptions  
- Users must manually contact landlords or agencies to confirm basic information  
- Administrative requirements such as **empadronamiento** are rarely clearly stated in listings  
- High competition requires very fast responses to new listings  
- Informal channels increase the risk of **housing scams or fraudulent listings**
